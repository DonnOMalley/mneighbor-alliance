# Hyper-Local Street Gathering Execution Matrix
*This document is the operational playbook used by an onboarding chapter to execute a safe, high-liability block gathering (including street closures, live music, potlucks, and high-risk equipment rentals).*

### Phase 1: Tool Selection & Data Privacy (45 Days Out)
The Protocol volunteer deploys the digital signature campaign to get block consent. Review the risk profile below to choose the utility that aligns with your Link's privacy parameters:

| Feature / Factor | 🔒 Option A: CryptPad Forms (Recommended) | 🌐 Option B: Google Forms (Alternative) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Data Privacy** | **Zero-Knowledge.** Client-side encrypted. The host server cannot read data. | **Commercial Data Core.** Subject to corporate data harvesting and profiling. |
| **User Experience** | **Frictionless Guest Access.** Sign via phone in 30 seconds with no account wall. | **Familiar Interface.** Ubiquitous feel, but can prompt users for a Google Login. |
| **The Primary Risk** | **Slight Volunteer Friction:** Spreadsheet export feels slightly less polished than corporate software. | **Privacy Degradation:** Feeds names and physical addresses into commercial data profiles. |

* **Option A Execution (CryptPad):** Launch a new form canvas on CryptPad.fr. Verify that Guest Access is toggled ON and Anonymize Responses is toggled OFF (the city requires verifiable names/addresses).
* **Option B Execution (Google Forms):** Open a standard form layout. Navigate to settings and explicitly turn OFF "Restrict to users in organization" and "Limit to 1 response" to prevent neighbors from hitting a mandatory account login wall.

### Phase 2: Permitting & Logistics Sequence (45 to 30 Days Out)
1. **Map the Target Block (45 Days Out):** Open Google Maps and take a clean screenshot of the exact street closure zone. Use a digital highlighter tool to clearly mark where the physical barricades will go. The Saint Paul Police Department (SPPD) Special Operations Unit explicitly rejects hand-drawn maps.
2. **Pull the Insurance Certificate (45 Days Out):** Log into the master MNeighbor Alliance insurance portal. Input your specific date and location boundaries to generate the official Certificate of Insurance (COI). Verify that the policy states a minimum of $1,000,000 in General Liability and that the text box explicitly names the City of Saint Paul as an additional insured.
3. **Circulate the Digital Petition (35 Days Out):** Print the standard Neighbor Link flyer featuring your chosen form's unique QR code. Drop a copy onto every porch within the designated Link boundaries. Neighbors scan and sign via phone. You must demonstrate a clear supermajority of household consent on the block.
4. **Submit to SPPD Special Operations (30 Days Out):** Download the Residential Block Club Application from the SPPD website. Export your signature spreadsheet as a clean PDF sorted by address. Email the Application, PDF Signature Ledger, Google Map diagram, and MNA Insurance Certificate to the SPPD Special Operations Unit. Submitting *before* the 30-day window completely waives the city's $50 processing fee.

### Phase 3: Day-of Execution Guidelines

#### 📦 Potluck Logistics & Safety
* The local Link provides the central core infrastructure (plates, napkins, ice, a main grilled item funded by the community chest).
* Set up food tables completely away from direct sunlight. Explicitly tag dishes containing common allergens (nuts, dairy, gluten).

#### 🎸 Live Music & Sound Management
* Locate an auxiliary outlet from an adjacent homeowner. Run heavy-duty, outdoor-rated extension cords to the performance area.
* **The Cord Shield (Mandatory Safety Action):** Every cord crossing a walking path must be covered by rubber industrial cord ramps or heavily taped down with gaffer tape to eliminate tripping hazards.
* Position amplifiers facing away from non-participating houses where possible, and ensure all amplified music strictly ceases prior to the local city noise ordinance curfew (10:00 PM).

#### 🏰 High-Risk Activities (Bounce Houses & Crafts)
* **The Bounce House Firewall:** Never sign an inflatable rental agreement under an individual neighbor's name. The contract must be executed by the master corporation: MNeighbor Alliance.
* The commercial rental company must provide a valid COI proving they hold active commercial liability coverage, explicitly naming MNeighbor Alliance as an additional insured.
* At least two adult volunteers must be physically stationed at the inflatable at all times to monitor capacity limits and keep older kids separated from toddlers.
* Setup low-profile folding tables for kid crafts (like button-making or face painting) entirely within the cordoned-off safety zone, safely removed from any active food-grilling equipment or hot surfaces.

#### 🧹 Clean-Up & Strike (Event Day + 2 Hours)
* Deploy clearly labeled trash and recycling stations at both ends of the block.
* Once the scheduled event window closes, break down the wood or plastic sawhorse barricades to immediately reopen the street to public emergency vehicle traffic. Never block the road with permanent or heavy personal vehicles.
