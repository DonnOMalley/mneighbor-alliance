# Corporate Registry & Fiduciary Architecture
*This document outlines the one-time setup required to build the master legal shield and financial hub for the alliance. Once these steps are complete, this master entity remains static and serves as the legal host for all subsequent neighborhood nodes.*

### 1. State Incorporation (MN Chapter 317A)
File a Domestic Nonprofit Corporation via the Minnesota Secretary of State online portal.
* **Legal Name:** MNeighbor Alliance
* **Statutory Rule:** File under Chapter 317A (Nonprofit Corporations).
* **Purpose Clause:** Must explicitly state: *"This corporation is organized exclusively for civic betterment and social welfare purposes under section 501(c)(4) of the Internal Revenue Code, specifically to foster hyper-local neighborhood community connection, safety, and mutual aid."*
* **Anti-Commercial Lock:** Include a core asset clause: *"No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure to the benefit of any private individual. Upon dissolution, all remaining corporate assets must be transferred directly to another non-profit neighborhood civic entity."*

### 2. Federal Identity & Tax Status
* **EIN Acquisition:** Immediately upon state approval, navigate to IRS.gov and apply for a free Employer Identification Number (EIN). Select "Social Club or Civic Association" as the entity structure.
* **501(c)(4) Self-Declaration:** Operating as a (c)(4) social welfare group allows the alliance to run local festivals and pool money without tracking individual charitable tax deductions or dealing with the strict operational limitations of a 501(c)(3).

### 3. Fiduciary Hub & Master Insurance
* **The Master Account:** Open a fee-free community business checking account at a local credit union using the approved MN State Articles of Incorporation and IRS EIN letter.
* **The Master Insurance Wrapper:** Secure an annual Commercial General Liability (CGL) Policy for small civic associations (target range: $600–$900/year). The policy must maintain a minimum limit of $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate to satisfy municipal street closure mandates.

### 4. Intellectual Property Lock
All digital assets, bylaws, guides, and materials generated by MNeighbor Alliance are natively stamped with a Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0) License. This legally guarantees that anyone can copy or modify the network frameworks, but nobody can ever sell them or commercialize the ecosystem.
