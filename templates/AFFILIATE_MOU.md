# AFFILIATE MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING (MOU)

**This Memorandum of Understanding (the "Agreement") is entered into by and between:**

**MNeighbor Alliance (MNA)**, a Minnesota nonprofit corporation, and 
**[Affiliate Name]**, a [Type of Organization, e.g., 501(c)(3) Nonprofit].

### 1. Purpose of Partnership
The purpose of this Agreement is to formalize a cooperative relationship to share resources, coordinate calendars, and improve neighborhood communication. Both parties agree that this is a partnership of equals dedicated to hyper-local civic improvement.

### 2. Fiduciary Firebreak (Liability Insulation)
Both MNeighbor Alliance and [Affiliate Name] remain separate, autonomous, and independent legal entities. Neither party assumes any financial, legal, or fiduciary liability for the actions, debts, events, or omissions of the other. No joint venture or agency relationship is created by this Agreement. 

### 3. Mesh Council Coordination
To ensure cross-neighborhood synchronization:
*   **The Router:** Each party shall appoint one primary point of contact ("The Router") to facilitate communication.
*   **The Sync:** The Routers agree to meet bi-annually (typically in the Spring and Fall) to synchronize event calendars, share street-closure permit insights, and discuss neighborhood-wide priorities.
*   **No Centralized Control:** Neither party has the authority to make decisions for, or commit resources on behalf of, the other party.

### 4. Resource Interoperability
**Shared Inventory:** Each party may designate equipment (e.g., traffic barricades, folding tables, audio/tech gear) as "Community Accessible."
**Access Protocols:** Lending of equipment shall be handled on an as-available, best-effort basis, with the borrowing party assuming responsibility for the return of the equipment in good working order.

### 5. Term and Termination
This Agreement is voluntary and non-binding. It shall remain in effect until terminated by either party. Either organization may withdraw from this MOU at any time, for any reason, by providing written notice (including email) to the other party's appointed Router.
